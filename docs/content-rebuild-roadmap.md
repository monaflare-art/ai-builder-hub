## 5. Main Quality Problems

### Placeholder Pattern

Many articles have the same section rhythm: who it is for, step-by-step process, workflow, what to look for, common mistakes, final recommendation, FAQ. This creates the impression of scaled AI content.

### Weak Evidence

Most pages do not show:

- Screenshots.
- Setup flows.
- Pricing verification.
- Product limitations.
- First-hand observations.
- Measured tests.
- Date-stamped review notes.

### Weak Differentiation

The articles often state reasonable advice, but not unique advice. They do not yet answer: why should this page rank over a larger competitor with real product screenshots?

### Weak Buyer Framing

Several pages mention tools but do not help the reader make a buying decision. Affiliate content needs clearer:

- Best for.
- Avoid if.
- Pricing table.
- Alternatives.
- Final decision path.
- CTA matched to user intent.

### Days 15-45: Rewrite P0 Pages

- Rewrite the top 10 P0 pages from `content-rebuild-priority.md`.
- Add product tables, real screenshots, current pricing checks, "avoid if" sections, and better CTAs.

### Days 46-75: Rewrite P1 Pages

- Rebuild hosting, domain, AI coding, SaaS, and launch tutorials that can support buyer journeys.
- Consolidate overlapping domain and traffic pages where necessary.

### Days 76-90: Cleanup and Consolidation

- Merge duplicate pages.
- Update internal links so authority flows toward Grade A and rebuilt P0/P1 pages.
- Create a tracking sheet for evidence freshness and pricing refresh dates.

## 8. Site Transformation Estimate

The site can become a high-quality affiliate property, but not by adding more articles. The path is:

1. Keep the 10 Grade A pages as the commercial foundation.
2. Rebuild the top 10 Grade B pages into evidence-backed buyer pages.
3. Merge or redirect the 7 Grade C pages after checking traffic.
4. Add real screenshots, setup notes, pricing checks, and update dates.

Best near-term outcome:
